Haleia's Career Reasearch on Cybersecurity Engineering

What is the Career?

Cybersecurity Engineerprotect computer systems and networks from cyber threats, such as hacking, malware, and data breaches. They develop and implement security measures to safeguard sensitive information and maintain the integrity of IT infrastructure. It involves a range of technologies, processes, and practices to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. Key areas include network security, information security, endpoint security, application security, operational security, and disaster recovery. Cybersecurity also focuses on continuous monitoring, threat detection, incident response, and educating users about safe practices to defend against various cyber threats like malware, phishing, and ransomware, ensuring the protection of digital assets.

What about that Career Interests You?

This career makes an exquisite amount of money, along with the good income it is a very secure and stable job that provides benefits for communities.Cybersecurity is also intriguing due to its dynamic and ever-evolving nature, requiring continuous learning to keep up with new technologies and threats. It operates at high stakes, protecting national security, financial stability, and personal privacy, making the work both urgent and impactful. The field combines advanced technology with human psychology, creating a complex blend of technical and behavioral understanding.

What Type of Education or Training is Needed for that Career?

Bachelor's degree in IT, systems engineering, computer science, or a field similar. Along with at least two years of cybersecurity work experience doing activities such as incident detection and forensics. Key certifications such as CompTIA Security+, CISSP, and CEH are essential for validating expertise. Practical experience through internships, lab work, and entry-level IT roles is crucial. Continuous learning via workshops, conferences, and online courses helps professionals stay updated with evolving threats and technologies. Strong analytical, detail-oriented, and communication skills are also important for effectively addressing security challenges..

What Other Interesting Information About the Field Did You Learn?

Cybersecurity is one of those fields that is always evolving. Their work revolves around the core of CIA Triad, incorporates encryptions which is a cutting-edge technology, and detection systems that are AI-driven. Cybersecurity is crucial for protecting information in our digital world, with costs of cybercrime projected to reach $10.5 trillion annually by 2025. Phishing remains the most common attack method, while AI is increasingly used to enhance defense mechanisms. The first computer virus, the "Creeper," emerged in the 1970s, and major breaches like Yahoo's in 2013 have exposed billions of user accounts. Despite rising threats, there is a significant shortage of cybersecurity professionals, with an estimated 3.5 million unfilled jobs by 2025.

How Will Artificial Intelligence Affect This Field?

AI will significantly impact cybersecurity in both positive and negative ways. On the positive side, AI can enhance cybersecurity by automating threat detection and response, analyzing vast amounts of data to identify patterns and anomalies indicative of cyber threats. It can predict and mitigate potential attacks before they occur and improve the accuracy and speed of detecting breaches. AI-driven tools can also assist in managing and prioritizing security alerts, reducing the burden on human analysts and allowing for a more efficient and proactive approach to cybersecurity. Cybercriminals can leverage AI to develop more sophisticated and harder-to-detect attacks, such as advanced phishing schemes, automated hacking, and the creation of polymorphic malware that changes its code to avoid detection. AI can also be used to analyze and exploit vulnerabilities more quickly than ever before.
